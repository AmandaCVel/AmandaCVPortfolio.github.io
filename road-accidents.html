Interactive Project:
Road Accidents in Europe

Here is a link for viewing the project online.

The Skills Used For This Project:

Excel- Pivot Tables, Line Chart, Bar Chart,
Data Cleaning- going through the data verify there are no duplicates, verify the data is spelled correctly, fixing minor and major mistakes, double check for blanks and deleting or dismissing them.
Data Processing- will apply some formulas and build some customized columns in the data, which will be used for the visualization.
Data Analysis- using the aggregation data to build different PivotTables (AKA PTs) and build charts out of them.
Data Visualization- building custom charts, use custom formatting, and then place them in the dashboard.

Steps For This Project:

  1. Requirement: gathering from client.
  2. Identify the Stakeholders of the Project.
  3. Data Cleaning as per the Requirements.
  4. Data Processing by adding some customized columns in the data/tables.
  5. Data analysis by Pivot Tables and Excel Functions.
  6. Data Visualization to create charts and custom sheets to show the insights.
  7. Report/Dashboard creation from start to end.

On the left in the light blue rectangle, you will find icons. The blue database icon will take you to the “Data Analysis Sheet.” The magenta (purple/red color) looks like a pie chart being presented, will take you to the “Dashboard” sheet. The green laptop icon will take you to the Wikipedia website where the data originated from. Finally, the purple mail icon, will ask you for your preferred email to send me an email. You will also find this light blue column with the same icons on that sheet as well.

This data contains the accidents that occurred in 2021 and 2022. The total casualties are 417,883. Underneath that you can see that the Fatal Casualties for both years 7,135 or 1.7%, for Serious Casualties 59,312 or 14.2%, and for Slight Casualties 351,436 or 84.1%. The highest Vehicle Type for all casualties is the car or personal vehicle at 333,485 or 79.8% of all Road Accidents.

Underneath the Fatal Casualties, you'll find the Casualties by Vehicle Type. They are in order from highest to lowest casualties; with cars or private vehicles being the most, different types of motorcycles second, third we have vans/goods, fourth we have busses, fifth is other, and lastly, would be the agricultural vehicles. To the right you can see the month-to-month casualties based on the year.

Next, there's the Casualties by Road Type, this chart shows the type of road that accident took place, whether it be a roundabout or one way.
Casualties by Road Surface which include Dry, Wet, or Snow/Ice. The white sliver next to Snow/Ice are the ones that were left blank.
The next set of charts, at the bottom row are the Casualties by Location and Casualties by Day/Night. Location is the Rural and Urban areas. The chart next to it displays what the lighting conditions were.
In the bottom right of the “Dashboard” sheet, “Filter Panel” you will find the “Accident Date” filter/timeline where you can filter the month(s) and year(s). The charts that are connected to this filter are all of them except for the other filter, “Urban/Rural” will filter out the data that has either happened in rural areas or urban areas. The charts that are connected to this filter are all of them.

The “Data Analysis Sheet” shows all the data that has been processed and cleaned then put into PivotTables. Client wants to create a Road Accident Dashboard for the years 2021 and 2022 so that they can have insight on the below requirements:

  • Primary KPI- Total Casualties taken place after the accident
  • Primary KPI's- Total Casualties & percentage of total with respect to accident severity and maximum casualties by type of vehicle
  • Secondary KPI's- Total Casualties with respect to vehicle type
  • Monthly trend showing comparison of casualties for Year 2022 and Year 2021
  • Maximum casualties by Road Type
  • Distribution of total casualties by Road Surface
  • Relation between Casualties by Area/Location & by Day/Night
